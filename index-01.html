About Holly

Holly Low and her husband Charlie moved to Frenchtown six years ago to start a family. She grew up in Long Branch and spent eight years fundraising for social justice nonprofits before leaving the workforce to parent full time. Holly is currently pursuing a Master’s of Public Policy from Rutgers University with a focus on Economics and Social Policy.

Holly’s public service in Frenchtown speaks to the enthusiasm, commitment, and “get it done” spirit that she would bring to Borough Council:

  • As an Environmental Commissioner since 2014, she helped secure $15,000 in grants to improve Boro Park’s nature trail and to update the environmental resources inventory. She also wrote the Commission’s official opposition to the PennEast pipeline, which threatens Frenchtown’s groundwater and recent streambank restoration along the Nishisakawick.
  • In 2013 she led the effort to raise over $5,000 to produce the highly successful community Green Fair, which connected residents and visitors to programs and speakers that advance environmental, economic, and social sustainability.
  • During her time with the Frenchtown Community Garden Holly wrote a successful grant to help protect the garden from flooding and coordinated donations of fresh produce to Homestead House.

As the mother of two boys, she wants to ensure that Frenchtown continues to be a wonderful place for children to grow up. Holly can help raise the funds we need to maintain and upgrade our treasured playground. With Sustainable Frenchtown, she’ll continue to coordinate events like the recent paper shredding and Riverfest bike valet, and support our certification as a Sustainable Jersey community.

With your support, Holly would like to take her public service to the next level and represent Frenchtown. She’ll work with the Mayor and Council to find ways to keep our taxes stable while maintaining the services we need. Holly is a strong supporter of buying local, and a passionate advocate for working families and people in need.
